## The Cast of Characters

### Parliament (120 MPs)
Elected by you every 3 years. Their jobs:
- Make laws
- Hold the Government to account
- Represent your interests

**Government** = The party (or coalition) with 61+ seats. They run the country.

**Opposition** = Everyone else. Their job is to question, challenge, and offer alternatives.

**Speaker** = The referee. An MP (usually from Government benches) who keeps order.

### The Executive (The Government)
- **Prime Minister** = Leader of the biggest party
- **Cabinet** = Senior ministers who make key decisions
- **Ministers** = Each runs a department (Health, Education, Finance, etc.)

## The Eight Steps Explained

### Step 1: Someone Has an Idea
- Usually a minister says "we need to fix X"
- Or an ordinary MP proposes a change (called a "Member's Bill")

### Step 2: It Becomes a Bill
- Lawyers draft the actual legal text
- Published publicly so anyone can read it

### Step 3: First Reading
- Parliament debates the *principle* â€” do we want this at all?
- Vote: 50% to continue
- Most bills pass this stage

### Step 4: Select Committee â­
- Bill goes to a small group of MPs (usually cross-party)
- **Public can submit opinions** (this is your chance to be heard)
- Committee can suggest changes
- **This is where most changes happen**

### Step 5: Second Reading
- Parliament debates the *details*
- More changes possible

### Step 6: Committee of the Whole House
- Every MP can suggest tweaks
- Line-by-line examination

### Step 7: Third Reading
- Final debate
- Final vote

### Step 8: Royal Assent
- Governor-General signs it (formality)
- Becomes law (now called an "Act")

## The Stuff Nobody Explains

### Question Time
- Every sitting day, 2-3pm
- Opposition MPs ask ministers questions
- Usually political theatre, sometimes genuinely revealing
- Watch live on [Parliament TV](https://www.parliament.nz/en/watch-parliament/)

### Urgent Debates
- If something major happens, Parliament can debate it immediately
- Requires the Speaker's approval

### Private Members' Bills
- Ordinary MPs (not ministers) can propose laws too
- Most fail, but some become major changes
- Example: Same-sex marriage started as a Member's Bill

### The Budget
- Once a year, Government reveals spending plans
- Huge debate, opposition responds with their alternative
